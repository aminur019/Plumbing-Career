Plumbing: A Respected & Essential Career

Choosing a career is a big step — and as a parent, you want your child to find a path that offers stability, growth, and meaning. Plumbing may not always be the first choice that comes to mind, but it offers exactly that.

Plumbers' Impact

  • Maintain safe water and sanitation systems
  • Support public health and infrastructure
  • Contribute to environmental sustainability
  • Are in consistent demand — locally and globally

Growth Potential

  • Specialize in green technology or gas fitting
  • Move into leadership or management
  • Run a business of their own

It's a respected profession that makes a positive difference — and offers financial stability and long-term opportunity.

Ideal Traits of a Plumber

If your child shows these traits, they may thrive in plumbing:

Problem-solving

Ability to assess situations and find solutions

Hands-on enjoyment

Prefers active, practical work

Attention to detail

Precision matters in plumbing systems

Good communication

Explaining issues and solutions clearly

Patience and perseverance

Plumbing can present complex challenges

Commitment to learning

The trade evolves — learning never stops

The Value of Practical Learning

Plumbing is learned by doing. Formal training provides theory — but real expertise comes from on-the-job experience.

If your child enjoys:

  • Dynamic, hands-on work
  • Solving tangible problems
  • Working in varied environments

Plumbing offers an ideal alternative to desk-based careers.

The Employment Challenge in Plumbing Training

One hurdle: You must be employed to complete your apprenticeship.

The "chicken-and-egg" dilemma: You need a job to gain experience, but experience helps you get a job.

Pre-apprenticeships

Build basic skills and stand out to employers

Own basic tools

Show you're ready to contribute

Right attitude

Enthusiasm, resilience, and work ethic matter

Networking

Build connections early — PIRB and IOPSA can help

Look for programs with job placement

Some schools offer this

Freelance where legal

Small jobs can count toward Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL)

Support your child in pursuing these steps — it improves their chances of success.

Long-Term Career Opportunities

Plumbing is not a "dead-end" job — it's a career with many growth paths.

Specialization

Move into high-demand fields:

  • Solar water heating
  • Gas fitting
  • Green plumbing solutions

Leadership & Management

Take on roles overseeing:

  • Crews
  • Large projects
  • Budgets and standards

Business Ownership

Many plumbers start their own businesses:

  • Flexible hours
  • Increased income potential
  • Independence and control

With dedication and continuous learning, plumbing offers a secure, prosperous future.

Supporting Your Child's Education, Training & Work Ethic

As a parent, you can help your child succeed by encouraging:

  • School subjects: Math, science, and technical courses
  • Practical training: Choose reputable, accredited institutions
  • Positive mindset: Foster resilience, responsibility, and adaptability

Success in plumbing requires:

  • Hard work
  • Continuous learning
  • A commitment to quality and professionalism

Your support in these areas can make a big difference.

Recognition & Growth Through Professional Bodies

Joining a body like PIRB strengthens your child's career:

Licensing

Adds credibility and trust with clients and employers

CPD

Keeps skills current and competitive in a changing industry

Networking

Access to mentors and industry peers for career growth

Being part of a respected professional community helps your child grow and succeed in a rapidly evolving trade.
